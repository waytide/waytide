# Say "examine", not "decode"

For working through or inspecting something to understand it, say **examine** — not "decode".

**Why:** "decode" is a figure — it treats a plain thing as a cipher to be cracked; "examine" states the plain act of looking at something to understand it. Literal naming over a figure (a case of the no-slang standard).

**How to apply:** in prose and dialogue, write "examine" (or "work through" / "inspect") where the impulse is "decode". Related: the name-literally-not-by-analogy rule and the no-slang rule.
