# Ask plainly — don't ask whether something "lands"

Don't ask whether something "lands" (e.g. "does that land?"). State it plainly: "is that right?", "does that work?", "is that correct?"

**Why:** "land" is a figure that dresses up a plain question; the direct form asks exactly what is meant with nothing to decode. Literal phrasing over slang (a case of the no-slang standard).

**How to apply:** in prose and conversation, replace "does that land?" and similar with a direct question — "is that right?", "does that work?". Related: the no-slang rule and the name-literally-not-by-analogy rule.
