# Say "impact", not "what it costs"

For the effects or consequences of a change, say **impact** — not "what it costs". Name them neutrally; "what it costs" presumes the effects are costs, when some may be neutral or beneficial.

**Why:** "what it costs" frames a change's consequences as a price paid; "impact" names them plainly, without presuming a cost. Literal, neutral naming over a loaded framing (a case of the name-literally-not-by-analogy standard).

**How to apply:** in prose, headings, summaries, and dialogue, write "impact" (or "effect" / "consequences") where the impulse is "what it costs". Related: the name-literally-not-by-analogy rule and the no-slang rule.
