# **Added the `say-obsolete-not-dead` rule to `vocabulary`** — "dead" (dead path, dead reference, dead link, dead code) is slang and over-dramatizes: nothing died, a reference whose target moved has simply stopped applying, which is what **obsolete** states literally. The exaggeration also distorts judgment, making a routine tidying read as urgent repair. Tenth one-substitution rule under the `no-slang` principle; the README's substitution table gains the row. **Consequence:** conformed this session's composite `agent/log/` entries, which had used "dead" throughout — and found that the published `say-verified-not-green` rule closed with "existing records are not retroactively **swept**", violating `vocabulary`'s own `say-conform-not-sweep`; now "retroactively conformed". Existing commit messages keep their words (they are historical records, and the earlier `code/ruby` trim commit was titled with "dead" before the rule existed).
