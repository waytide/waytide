# Expand an abbreviation on first use, or don't use it

Do not use an abbreviation the reader may not already know without spelling it out. On its own, "HITL" or "DBE" tells the reader nothing until they have seen what it stands for.

- **Introduce it once, then use it.** The first time an abbreviation appears — in a document or in conversation — write the full term with the abbreviation in parentheses: "human-in-the-loop design (HITL)". After that, the short form is fine within the same document or exchange.
- **Prefer the full term when in doubt.** Use an abbreviation only when it is genuinely more recognizable than what it stands for (for example "AI", "URL", "HTTP"). If the expansion is clearer than the short form, just use the expansion.
- **Project abbreviations count too.** A name coined inside the project — DBE for design-by-efferent — is defined where it is introduced (the design-by-efferent vocabulary) and used freely only after that.

**Why:** an unexplained abbreviation forces the reader to guess or go look it up. It saves the writer a few letters and costs the reader the meaning — the same failure as leading with jargon the reader may not share. Spelling it out once removes the guesswork for good.

**How to apply:** the first time an abbreviation appears, write the full term with the abbreviation in parentheses; use the short form afterward. Skip the expansion only for abbreviations more widely known than their expansions. Applies to conversation with the user as much as to the files.
