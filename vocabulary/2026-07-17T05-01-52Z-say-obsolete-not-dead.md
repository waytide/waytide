# Say "obsolete", never "dead"

For a reference, path, term, rule, or artifact that no longer applies — one whose target has moved, been renamed, or been retired — say it is **obsolete**. Never "dead" ("dead path", "dead reference", "dead link", "dead code").

**Why:** "dead" is slang, and it over-dramatizes — it imports a finality and a drama the situation does not have. Nothing died: a reference whose target moved has simply stopped applying, which is what **obsolete** states literally. The exaggeration also distorts judgment, making a routine tidying read as urgent repair. Literal naming over a figure of speech (a case of the no-slang standard).

**How to apply:** write "obsolete" wherever the impulse is "dead", in names, prose, comments, commit messages, and reports. Where a more exact word is available, prefer it — a reference can be **unresolvable**, a rule **retired**, an artifact **superseded**. Related: the no-slang rule and the name-literally-not-by-analogy rule.
