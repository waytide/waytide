# Say "scenario", never "arm"

For one variant or case of a method or feature, say **scenario** — never "arm". "the name scenario", "the module scenario", not "the name arm".

**Why:** "arm" is a figure borrowed loosely; "scenario" states what is meant — one of the distinct cases a method or feature handles. Literal naming over slang (a case of the no-slang standard).

**How to apply:** call a variant or case of a method/feature a "scenario", in names, prose, comments, and commit messages. Related: the no-slang rule and the name-literally-not-by-analogy rule.
