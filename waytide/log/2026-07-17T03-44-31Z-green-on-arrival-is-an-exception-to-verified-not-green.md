# **"Green-on-arrival" is an exception to the `say-verified-not-green` rule** and keeps its name — the `no-green-on-arrival-tests` rule, and the compound wherever it appears in the DBE rules. The verified-not-green rule governs *reporting that tests pass*: "green" as the tooling-flavored metaphor standing in for "the behavior is confirmed," which "verified" states directly. "Green-on-arrival" is not that report — it names a **state in the red/green cycle**: the test arrives already passing, so no red drives it, so it designs nothing. The red/green frame is what makes the name mean anything, and the same reasoning keeps the **red/green loop** its name. The rule's blanket sentence — "The compound 'green-on-arrival' likewise becomes 'verified-on-arrival'" — reaches past its own rationale and does not bind here; the `testing` package's rule wants that sentence amended to match (a published package, so gated).
