# Established the discriminator for "TDD" vs "DBE" labeling: DBE comprises TDD, so referring to TDD is fine where it genuinely names TDD-the-genus (the general red-green practice DBE builds on) — but anything labeled "TDD" that is not strictly general TDD, and would otherwise fall into DBE, is relabeled DBE. The test per item: is this strictly general TDD, or DBE wearing a TDD label? Applied selectively (not "all TDD → DBE", which would erase the genus, and not "defer", which leaves DBE mislabeled): most `tdd-*` methodology rules are DBE-specific (cradle/solubility/efferent/hinge machinery) and relabel to DBE; `tdd-lexicon` is the DBE lexicon; "TDD" is kept only where it names the general cycle (chiefly the design-vs-coverage distinction). This sharpens the design-by-efferent name rule ("TDD is the genus, DBE is the name") and governs the design-by-efferent increment's conformance pass.
