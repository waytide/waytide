# Added `expand-abbreviations-on-first-use` to the `language` package — a rule against unexplained abbreviations: spell an abbreviation out the first time it appears (full term, abbreviation in parentheses), use an abbreviation only when it is more recognizable than its expansion, and define project abbreviations (e.g. DBE) where they are introduced. Prompted by using "HITL" for human-in-the-loop with no expansion. It is a fourth principle rule beneath the `language-is-precise-here` premise, sharpening `communicate-plainly-name-the-thing` (an unexplained abbreviation is shorthand the reader may not share). The language README's discipline list goes from three to four. Adds to the pending `language` re-publish.
