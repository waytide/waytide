# Renamed hinge 2 from **assertion** to **observation**. The hinge is the decision of what to observe about the outcome of the actuation (value-equality vs. identity vs. a raised error; what the explaining variable is compared against); an **assertion** is only the mechanism by which an observation is implemented. This applies the test-triad decision (control → actuate → observe, not → assert) to the hinge cycle, and completes the `hinges-gate-the-design-of-a-test` rule's transitional "assertion/observation" to plain **observation**. Added `observation` to the DBE vocabulary as a term. Scope is the hinge's *name*: a rule speaking at the level of the test file's mechanics still says "assert" (that is what the code does — e.g. testing's `tdd-test-structure`, "assert each outcome in its own nested context"), and prose using "asserted" descriptively ("what is asserted about the result") stands. Renamed rules: `hinge-cycle`, `what-each-hinge-displays`, `hinges-gate-the-design-of-a-test`, `dbe-first-turn-test-file`.
