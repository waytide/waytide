# **Authored `language-is-precise-here` as the package's governing principle** — the premise `name-literally`, `no-slang`, and every substitution rule follow *from*, which the package had never stated. Under this model the use of language is very precise: a word is not a label applied to a thought after the fact, it is the thought's working surface, so choosing a word chooses a concept and the words are load-bearing. **Two things the rule pins down.** *Precision is not formality* — elaborate, technical, careful-sounding language is usually the opposite, adding ceremony while saying less; the most precise word is very often the plainest one. And imprecise words *do damage, quietly*: a metaphor carries baggage the author never intended, an overdramatic word distorts judgment (a routine tidying reads as urgent repair), a loose synonym silently merges two concepts the design depends on keeping apart — and by the time the confusion surfaces, its origin is a word chosen carelessly some distance back. The rule directs that a word choice be treated as a design decision, and that a word found wrong be changed everywhere the concept appears.
