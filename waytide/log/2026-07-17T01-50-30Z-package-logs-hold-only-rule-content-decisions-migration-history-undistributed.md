# Refined the per-package-log convention: a `<package>/log/` (which distributes to consumers when the package is installed) carries **only genuine design decisions about that package's rule content** — provenance a consumer benefits from — and **migration, packaging, and authoring history is kept undistributed** in the composite repo's top-level `agent/log/`, which is never split or pushed. Shipping packaging-system decisions (the `include:`/`package.md` convention, composite/component naming, the ISO-prefix exemption, per-package-log placement, a package's build/relocation) into a distributed package log would push build trivia to every consumer, who wants the rules and their genuine rationale, not our history. Consequences: the four packaging entries stay in the composite log; the `git/log/` `subject-first` re-home entry (migration trivia referencing `constant`'s old folders) was moved back to the composite log and the git package re-split and pushed without it; `foundation/log/` starts empty and gains an entry only on a real design choice about a foundation rule. This supersedes the earlier plan to relocate the packaging entries into `foundation/log/`.
