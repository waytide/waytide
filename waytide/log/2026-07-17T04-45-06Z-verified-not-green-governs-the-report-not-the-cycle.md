# **Narrowed `say-verified-not-green` to the *report*, carving out the red/green cycle.** The rule had said "The compound 'green-on-arrival' likewise becomes 'verified-on-arrival'" — a blanket extension that reached past the rule's own rationale. The rationale is that "green" is a tooling-flavored metaphor standing in for *the behavior is confirmed*, which "verified" states directly. **Green-on-arrival is not that claim**: it names a state in the red/green cycle — a test that arrives already passing, so no red drives it, so it designs nothing. The red/green frame is what makes the name mean anything, and "verified-on-arrival" says less, not more. The rule now states the scope (the report) and names the two standing exceptions: **green-on-arrival** and the **red/green loop**. This unblocks the `design-by-efferent` package, which keeps `no-green-on-arrival-tests` under that name.
