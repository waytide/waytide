# **The `tdd-lexicon` rule became the DBE `vocabulary.md`** — the package's cohesive glossary of interlocking terms, not a set of one-rule-per-term files. The lexicon's terms are defined by their relations to each other (the one-liner defines five in a sentence; "subtle" and "load-bearing" mean nothing except as the two marks of a hinge, and the argument that a hinge needs *both* belongs to neither term alone; the distributed-OODA framing frames the whole set), so splitting it would have destroyed what makes it a lexicon. This is the carve-out the packaging design provided for, and it draws the line against the M3 precedent principledly: `vocabulary`'s substitutions are one-rule-each because each swap is **independent**; a cohesive glossary of interlocking terms is a different artifact. It absorbs the M4 folds (efferent, actuation), carries the DBE name entry (retiring "efferent-oriented design"), adds **observation** as a term, and **references** `solubility` in the `vocabulary` package rather than defining it.
